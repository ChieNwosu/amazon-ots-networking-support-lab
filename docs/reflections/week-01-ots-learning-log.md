## Why I Am Building This Repository

This repository exists to bridge the gap between my Amazon Fulfillment Center operations experience and the technical skills required for an IT Support Engineer I, Ops Tech Solutions (OTS) role. Working on the FC floor, I see daily how network and device availability directly impacts operational throughput. When systems go down, packages stop moving. That firsthand awareness of uptime requirements motivates me to develop the technical skills to be the person who restores connectivity, diagnoses root causes, and documents solutions.

The documentation-first approach is intentional. OTS engineers write runbooks, follow SOPs, communicate technical findings, and document incidents. By building this skill in parallel with networking knowledge, I am preparing for both the technical and communication aspects of the role simultaneously.

## How Amazon FC Operations Connects to OTS

My current FC experience provides:

- **Uptime awareness:** I understand what happens operationally when IT systems fail. This is not theoretical for me.
- **Process discipline:** Following SOPs, documenting deviations, and communicating status are daily expectations in FC operations. These same disciplines define IT support work.
- **Prioritization under pressure:** FC operations run on tight timelines. I already practice triaging based on what is blocking the most work right now.
- **Physical environment familiarity:** I know the FC layout, the types of devices in use, and the environmental conditions that affect equipment. This context helps OTS engineers locate problems faster.

What FC experience does not provide is hands-on technical troubleshooting depth. That is exactly what the Coursera coursework and this learning lab are designed to build.

## First Technical Focus Areas

This week I am beginning to study:

- **OSI model:** Understanding how network communication is layered helps structure troubleshooting from physical to application layer
- **TCP/IP protocol suite:** The foundation of all network communication in enterprise environments
- **DNS:** Name resolution is critical for all application access; DNS failures are high-frequency support issues
- **DHCP:** IP address assignment failures are common in high-density device environments like FCs
- **Subnetting:** Understanding network segmentation enables faster isolation of connectivity issues
- **Basic endpoint troubleshooting:** Workstation triage, NIC diagnostics, and OS-level network verification

These topics align directly with OTS daily responsibilities: diagnosing why a device cannot reach the network, why a workstation cannot resolve names, or why a scanner is not getting an IP address.
